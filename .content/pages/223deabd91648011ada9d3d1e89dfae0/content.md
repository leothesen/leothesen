We arrive at the door and there’s a small dog yapping - why are the Portuguese obsessed with small yappy dogs? The door is open the the lock box is too, key inside. We open the main door and cautiously call out “ola?” (Which I realizes is the name of the ice cream brand). We call the number written outside and they answer. A woman and her son come out (we initially thought that it was a sugar mommy kind of vibe) and they welcome us in. It’s a beautiful fancy house with the upstairs rooms available for pilgrims. We welcome comforts, an en suite bathroom and beautiful view with a great bed. Tired bodies, happy toes.

We head down to the local restaurant for dinner. Have a few really nice conversations with people there, who talk of their life here: it seems they mostly work in Porto and have moved out here to raise families. The chef gets involved with serving our table as she speaks better English and we’re well looked after.

The sleep was incredible. 
## Cait’s Strava Entry
The day 3 scaries got us! 🙃
A very slow start was necessary after the slight rush of yesterday. Packing up was made cuter by the presence of the weird campsite cat who finally warmed up to us. After more heavy bag adjustment and optimisation, a small very yappy dog sent us on our way.
Leo got his breakfast beer, it just happened to be around lunchtime. We ate an enormous meal, courtesy of the lovely woman working at the restaurant, and set off.
We struggled a lot today. Leo’s bag is probably in the high 20s or low 30s of kilograms (we have yet to find a scale) and I’ve never carried before (and I’m in my luteal phase oh joy 🙃). We sloooowly plodded until we decided to make it a shorter day, after an incredibly sweet and heartwarming visit to a small church in Marinhas. We saw lots of cats, beautiful cobblestone streets, and received 10 “Bom Caminhos”. Settled in Belinho at a very comfy spot, had a yummy dinner and relationship check in, and managed to fall asleep before midnight ‼️
