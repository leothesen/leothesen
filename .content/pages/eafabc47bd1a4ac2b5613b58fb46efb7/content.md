Food was exceptional. You loaded “Klingaling” onto a wrist band to pay for everything. 1 Klingaling = 1 Euro, which after a few days lost its meaning and suddenly 12 Klingaling for a delicious Tagine felt like a bargain. Saving the wallet involved some amazing picnics that we carried around with us all day.

Trash? None of it. A 1 Klingaling cup deposit meant that finding a cup on the floor was the equivalent of a R20 note - so it was a rarity. Bins were great and the dancefloors were spotless.

Compost toilets worked perfectly. A much better experience to the Boland toilet hire potties that we were used to.

The crowd was unlike anything I had experienced before, and after consulting with a few people who had been to more European/UK festivals than I had, apparently it’s the norm. Dancefloor etiquette was observed at *all* times. There was not a moment where I felt bothered, or didn’t have space, where someone wasn’t conscious of me stepping around or past them. People made eye contact, thanked each other, smiled warmly. There was also no pretense, no “look at me for I am so cool”. I was reminded of a conversation that I had once with a dear friend, a dancer and raver among *many* other things, where we both agreed that when we’re on a dancefloor it’s as though we are at church. Some people go to a building and kneel, we find prayer in the movement and energy, the sway of bodies, the weightlessness and deep presence.
