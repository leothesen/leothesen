Through the scorching heat of the day we wonder further along to a small cafe for lunch. It’s a smaller residential suburb. We enter in a pool of sweat and the waiter shows us utter ambivalence. A burger later and we head back towards our house for a siesta. 
<empty-block/>
It’s been such a lovely visit with Courtnay - she’s on very much the same wavelength as us by way of relaxation first. We take the day very slowly. After a sleep we head off to dinner: tapas on the street. We try the local octopus delicacy, I can’t say that I’m such a fan. I feel sad eating octopus, having befriended one or two as a child in Knysna. 
<empty-block/>
We wonder down the beautiful streets in the late night light. Music calls us around a corner and there is a child brass band playing covers of Queen songs (among others). Two young twins are less interested in the music and cause a small, hilarious scene in front of us.

We amble back towards home and come across a small bar with people in the street. An elderly drunk woman is talking passionately with a man, her old person wheeled basket at her side. As we pass her she lurches towards us and unleashes a growling snort with a demonic tone at us - we scream and laugh and continue walking. I’m uncertain as to what she meant to do, I’ve come up with the following options:
1. She wanted to scare us
2. She forgot how to speak in the moment, tried to say “buenas noches” but breathed in instead of out, which made the snort. 
3. Was possessed by a demon.

We end off the night reading hilarious Spanish titles of well known Netflix shows: SpongeBob SquarePants becomes Bob Esponja and Squid Game becomes El juego del calamar. And then settling on the documentary about the cruise liner that all went wrong. 
<empty-block/>
In the morning we have a slow breakfast and stroll down to the train station, have a drink and say farewell to Courtnay and a perfect chilled weekend. Cait and I train to Vigo for the night where we’re going to start the Camino from tomorrow and walk to Santiago. Feeling excited and rested.
